Write for Clause & Capital

We publish rigorous, original analysis from practitioners, academics, and emerging voices at the intersection of law, economics, and business.

Who We Publish

We welcome contributions from barristers, solicitors, economists, finance professionals, academics, and policy specialists. Your perspective must be grounded in expertise and original analysis — not opinion dressed as fact.

What We Pay

We currently operate as a prestige platform without contributor fees. Contributors receive editorial support, a professional author profile, and an audience of senior UK professionals. Paid contributions are planned for 2027.

Word Count

Long-form analysis: 1,500–3,000 words. Briefings and commentary: 600–900 words. We do not publish op-eds under 600 words. All submissions are edited for clarity and house style.

Response Time

We aim to respond to all pitches within 10 working days. Commissioned pieces are typically published within three weeks of final submission. We do not accept simultaneous submissions.

Submission Guidelines

  • Pitches should include a working title, a 150-word summary, and your credentials/affiliation.
  • Full drafts are accepted but a pitch is preferred — it saves both parties time.
  • All claims must be properly sourced; we follow OSCOLA citation style for legal content.
  • Pieces must be original and not published or under consideration elsewhere.
  • We do not publish content that promotes specific legal or financial products or services.
  • Authors retain copyright; we request first UK digital publication rights.
  • We reserve the right to decline, edit, or retitle any submission.

About the Review
About Clause & Capital

A publication at the serious intersection of law, economics, and British commercial life.

Our Mission

Clause & Capital was founded in 2024 on a simple conviction: that law, economics, and business are not separate disciplines but interlocking forces that shape every corner of British professional and commercial life. Most publications treat them separately. We treat them as one.

We publish long-form analysis, not commentary. We cite sources, not opinions. We write for practitioners, not spectators — the solicitor advising on a cross-border acquisition, the economist modelling labour market reform, the CFO navigating ESG disclosure requirements.

Independence

Clause & Capital accepts no advertising, no sponsored content, and no placement fees. We are entirely reader-supported. No law firm, financial institution, or commercial interest has any editorial influence over our output. Our contributors are selected for expertise, not for access.

How We Started

The publication grew out of a series of long-form notes shared between three professionals — a barrister, an economist, and a strategy consultant — who wanted to read the kind of analysis that existed in academic journals but was written in plain, precise English for a professional audience. That ambition became Clause & Capital.

Standards & Practice
Editorial Policy

Our commitment to accuracy, independence, and rigour.

Editorial Independence

Clause & Capital is editorially independent. No advertiser, sponsor, law firm, financial institution, or third party has any influence over our editorial decisions. Our contributors are selected on the basis of expertise and analytical quality alone.

Accuracy & Corrections

We take factual accuracy seriously. All articles are reviewed before publication. If you believe an article contains a factual error, please contact us at corrections@clauseandcapital.com. Corrections are published prominently and transparently.

Sources & Citations

All legal content follows OSCOLA citation style. Economic and financial data is sourced from publicly available datasets (ONS, Bank of England, OBR, IMF). Contributors are required to provide source documentation on request.

Conflicts of Interest

Contributors are required to disclose any relevant conflicts of interest prior to publication. Where a conflict exists that cannot be mitigated, the piece will not be published. We do not publish pieces that promote specific legal or financial products.

AI Policy

Clause & Capital does not publish AI-generated articles. Contributors may use AI tools as research aids, but all analysis and writing must be the original work of the named author. We reserve the right to ask contributors to demonstrate original authorship.

Comments & Responses

We do not operate a public comments section. Substantive responses to published articles may be submitted for consideration as a right-of-reply piece, subject to our editorial standards.

Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your data.

Data We Collect

When you subscribe to our newsletter, we collect your name and email address. This is the only personal data we collect. We do not use cookies for tracking, advertising, or analytics. We do not collect IP addresses or browsing behaviour.

How We Use Your Data

Your name and email are used solely to send you the Clause & Capital Weekly Intelligence Briefing. We do not share your data with any third party, ever. We do not sell data. We do not use it for any purpose other than sending you the briefing you subscribed to.

Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to access, correct, or delete your personal data at any time. To unsubscribe, click the unsubscribe link in any email. To request deletion of your data, email privacy@clauseandcapital.com and we will action it within 48 hours.

Data Storage

Subscriber data is stored securely in a Supabase database hosted in the EU. Data is encrypted at rest and in transit. We retain subscriber data only for as long as you remain subscribed.

For any privacy-related queries, contact privacy@clauseandcapital.com.
